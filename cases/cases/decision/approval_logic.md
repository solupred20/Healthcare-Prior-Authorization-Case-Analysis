# Prior Authorization Decision Framework

## Objective
Provide a structured approach to evaluate prior authorization requests based on clinical necessity, guidelines, and utilization management principles.

---

## Decision Workflow

1. Verify Diagnosis
- Is the diagnosis clearly documented?
- Is it supported by clinical findings?

2. Review Requested Service
- Medication, procedure, or diagnostic test
- Is it standard of care for this condition?

3. Check Clinical Guidelines
- Does the request align with evidence-based guidelines?
- Is the service indicated at this stage of the condition?

4. Evaluate Previous Treatment
- Has first-line therapy been attempted?
- Was there treatment failure or contraindication?

5. Assess Medical Necessity
- Will the service improve patient outcomes?
- Is it required to prevent complications?

6. Identify Red Flags
- Severe symptoms
- Rapid progression
- Emergency indicators

---

## Approval Criteria

Approve if:
- Diagnosis is valid and supported
- Treatment follows clinical guidelines
- Prior treatments have failed (if applicable)
- Medical necessity is clearly justified

---

## Denial Criteria

Deny if:
- Insufficient clinical evidence
- Request is premature
- No prior conservative treatment attempted
- Service not aligned with guidelines

---

## Escalation Criteria

Escalate to medical reviewer if:
- Case is complex or unclear
- Conflicting clinical data
- High-risk patient condition

---

## Documentation Requirements

Each decision must include:
- Clear decision (Approved / Denied / Escalated)
- Clinical rationale
- Reference to guidelines (if applicable)
- Recommended next steps

---

## Quality Considerations

- Ensure consistency across cases
- Avoid bias in decision making
- Maintain patient safety as top priority
- Follow payer policies and clinical standards
